Cloud Computing

1. What does it do? What is the state of the art of this new technology? What can be done now? What is likely to be able to do be done soon (say in the next 3 years)? What technological or other developments make this possible?

Cloud computing refers to the use of remote servers on a network used to store, manage, and process data on the ‘cloud’. Without cloud computing, companies would have to spend resources on expensive physical IT infractures and maintenance. However, with cloud computing, it allows companies to simply access a variety of services such as computing power, servers and storage over the internet whenever it is needed - thus the term, “on demand”. It is relatively resource efficient as companies are only required to pay for what they only need, minimising potential excess resources typically associated with maintaining physical hardware. There are generally 4 types of cloud deployments which include the public, private, hybrid, and community cloud.

Public clouds examples include those offered by Microsoft, Google, and Amazon. Essentially, with public clouds, infrastructure and services are located on the establishment of a separate company that offers cloud computing services. Public clouds tend to have an abundance of available storage which naturally correlates to high and easy scalability. The services and infrastructure on public clouds provided by cloud computing companies are typically shared by all customers.

Private clouds differ to public clouds in how computing infrastructure and services are hosted, owned, and utilized only by a single organization - not being shared with any other party or establishment. Although less economical, private clouds offer higher levels of control and security as they are situated behind a protective firewall. Additionally, since private clouds are operated and hosted by the company itself, the risk of sudden external changes affecting the entire infrastructure is significantly reduced.

Hybrid clouds are often described to provide the best of both aspects and services of public and private clouds. Hybrid clouds offer the large scalability of a public cloud with the high level of security and control of a private cloud. Hypothetically, an organisation could have their most important and sensitive data stored and encrypted behind their firewall before securely transitioning to a more public environment when necessary.

Community clouds are shared between organisations or specific associations that share a common goal. Community clouds allow for a relatively easy way to achieve an easy-to-access and a highly functional collaborative environment to achieve a shared goal. Despite its sizable history, cloud computing can be considered to still be in its early stages with multiple years of growth yet to uncover. Technological advances in services such as video streaming platforms and augmented reality are becoming highly relevant in today’s modern age. Considering a potential external technological breakthrough in the future, it could result in a very high demand in the further development of cloud computing technology.

Augmented reality is often referred to as 'AR'. To put it simply, it is a way to display a picture on your device but in your real environment. Usually on a phone, it uses the phone's camera to capture the environment around the user (in real time) and displays on the screen what the user wants to see. At the moment, there are a few different implications of AR. A very common example is the Ikea's AR app (IKEA Place) where users are able to use their phone to see how a piece of furniture would look in their very own home before purchasing it in store. A similar function is also seen in the global e-market, Amazon. These apps are very useful for planning and redecorating purposes. Contrary to popular belief of Augmented Reality being a technology of the future, it has existed since the 1990s. According to Emspak (2018), Augmented reality was used to show information such as speed, altitude and direction of the fighter jet on the pilot’s heads-up displays. Augmented reality is a technology that is integrated into many aspects in a person’s life through different programs. However, the technology is not developed well enough to provide consumers with affordable gadgets that use AR. One of the most popular tech equipment that uses AR is Google’s ‘Google Glass’, which was popular for its functionality but ultimately did not gain much traction due to its high price point. However, mobile app developers saw an opportunity to use AR for their products. Current smartphones are much more capable of handling technology such as Augment Reality than even the desktops or laptops were able to when AR was in its early age. Therefore, it can be concluded that Augmented Reality is still in a late development stage.

There are several uses for Augmented Reality in the present day. These uses are mainly done through a smartphone. As mentioned before, companies such as Amazon and IKEA have utilized Augmented Reality to give their customers a virtual view of how the product will look like in their home. Augmented Reality has also been integrated into the medical sector. AR is used in MRI equipment as well as when performing surgeries. According to Paine (2020), medical students at the Cleveland Clinic at Case Western Reserve University, have the ability to learn human anatomy via an AR headset. Though these are popular uses of AR, the most popular use of AR would be its integration into the gaming world. As mentioned before, for an everyday person, AR is most conveniently available to them through their smartphones. In recent times, smartphone or mobile gaming has gained a lot of popularity as smartphones become cheaper and powerful. The world saw the capability of Augmented Reality in 2016, when the game giant Nintendo released ‘Pokemon Go’. This game had heavy Augmented reality elements in the core game-play. Chamary (2018), has stated that Pokemon Go managed to be the mobile game that earned the fastest $100 million (though it was released as a free-to-play game) and the most downloaded in the first month of release. All of this proves the capability of Augmented Reality when it is implemented well.

Augmented reality is a technology that still has a lot of room to improve. Thus, it is safe to assume that in the next few years there would be a lot more real-world uses for Augmented Reality. Proper implementation of Augmented Reality will make improvements in any and every field. Doctors would be able to see their patient’s anatomy in real time. Islam (2018) states that AR can be used to display patients’ anatomy as well as guide surgeons in doctors with extreme accuracy in surgeries. On top of that people who are looking to buy a house will be able to view the particular house as if they were there. Early stages of this example have already allowed customers to access a 360° view of the house they want to buy. Therefore, it can be concluded that the near future of AR is quite promising.

Cyber security is an added blanket of protection against cyberattacks. Cyber security blocks attackers from accessing critical information from someone. Cyberattacks are usually aimed towards businesses and people. This usually is to collect personal information about a particular user/business and to extort money from them. This can interrupt businesses by breaching their security and stopping a business from running. It challenges a business's integrity when a business that collects personal information gets a security breach in this way. Breaches in cyber security can result in loss of important data, identity theft and extortion. The most common cyber security threats are as follow: ransomware; malicious software that is designed to extort money by blocking access to particular files without the user paying up a fee to access these files (not always recoverable), phishing; fraudulent emails that appear as reputable sources, these are usually aimed to steal sensitive data such as credit card and login information, malware; software designed to damage a computer or to gain authorisation access, social engineering; using tactics to trick someone into giving their personal information away to give the attacker access to their sensitive information.

Machine learning Machine learning is providing computers the ability to think needless of programming and allowing computers to learn automatically without human intervention or assistance. ML incorporates various technologies creating systems that learn from data in its environment and produces informed outcomes. There are 4 main types of machine learning: supervised machine learning, unsupervised machine learning, reinforcement machine learning and deep learning.

Supervised machine learning: This type of machine learning requires feedback data from a user. It tries out an algorithm and then waits for feedback on whether or not it is done correctly, to initiate more tries. Using methods such as regression, classification, prediction and gradient boosting to observe similar patterns and predict future outcomes. E.g It can predict a fraudulent credit card transaction.

Unsupervised machine learning: This type of machine learning differs from supervised by not requiring feedback from a user. The machine will continuously run regardless of feedback being inputted, unless it is hard coded. E.g consumer trends (clustering customers into similar groups based on their behaviours and characteristics) and email spam detection.

Semi-supervised Machine learning: This is a blend of both supervised and unsupervised learning algorithms, and they have the ability to combine a little measure of labelled data along with a larger set of unlabeled data, this reduces the burden of getting more labelled data.

Reinforcement machine learning: This type of machine learning is similar to supervised machine learning however it is not trained using sample data. The model uses trial and error to complete tasks until the feedback from a user comes positive. This method allows machines to improve their accuracy and performance at tasks.

Deep learning: This is a method of machine learning that emulates a human brain. It has layers of successful supervised machine learning and continually adjusts and improves its outcomes. Deep learning is currently used to identify objects in images and words in sounds. Soon enough, these methods of pattern recognition will be used for speech recognition, medical diagnosis purposes as well as other recognising other business problems.
